Introduction

I've created a few videos that deal with customizing the World of Warcraft game interface. Those videos assume a certain familarity with the game but not with programming or interface design. Throughout the series you'll learn how to incorporate modifications and enhancements (called addons) that other players have made into your own custom UI. You'll also learn about WeakAuras: a powerful addon that acts more like a framework for creating and distributing your own mini addons. As you learn how to use the advanced features of WeakAuras you'll being writing your own addons: first by modifying WeakAuras with Lua and Blizzard’s Warcraft API, and then by extracting your custom functions into stand-alone addons. Learning to program by modifying a game you already play means you can work on useful projects right away, without getting too caught up in learning the basics.

WeakAuras is a powerful and flexible framework that allows you to display highly customizable graphics on your screen to indicate buffs, debuffs, and a whole host of similar types of information.
